Meera was proud of her portfolio optimizer. It combined 25 technical indicators, weighted them using historical correlation matrices, and dynamically rebalanced every week. 
On paper, it was unbeatable. Backtests showed smooth equity curves and minimal volatility. But when she stress-tested the system on out-of-sample data, performance collapsed.
The culprit was subtle. Her optimization routine selected parameters that performed best over the last 10 years. However, many of those parameters were tuned to rare events — specific volatility spikes and sector rotations that never repeated.
Her system wasn’t predictive. It was memorizing history. Meera reduced the number of indicators, simplified weighting logic, and introduced cross-validation. The performance dropped slightly in backtests but became far more stable in forward simulations.
She learned that simplicity often survives reality better than complexity.
