Rohan managed a mid-sized hedge fund. His analysts relied heavily on momentum signals and volatility filters. Every model looked impressive during trending markets.
Then came a sudden liquidity shock. Spreads widened. Slippage increased. Execution costs doubled.
The models had assumed frictionless markets. While signal accuracy remained high, realized returns dropped dramatically due to transaction costs and market impact. The backtests had ignored real-world execution constraints
Rohan introduced realistic assumptions:
Slippage modeling
Transaction cost adjustments
Position size limits based on liquidity
The Sharpe ratio dropped from 2.1 to 1.3.
But it was honest. He realized that risk doesn’t just live in price movements — it lives in assumptions.
